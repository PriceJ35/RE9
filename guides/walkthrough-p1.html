RE9 Walkthrough Part 1 — Sanatorium: Grace's Escape

Source: entertainment14.net / Ali213

Sawara Hotel — The Investigation Begins

Note: I'll try to avoid major story spoilers. Any spoiler content will be marked in red. (Jump scare moments won't be warned either — it's more fun to experience them yourself.)

Game Settings & Controls

Before starting the game, the system will have you configure some basic settings like language, voice, and brightness. Adjust these to your preference.

There are two playable protagonists this time. Grace's default perspective is first-person, while Leon's is third-person. If you're not comfortable with either, you can switch at any time during gameplay.

If you struggle with aiming, you can turn on Aim Assist.

After finishing all settings, you'll choose a difficulty. I selected Standard (Modern) here. Choosing Classic will restrict some of Grace's auto-saves, but otherwise there's no difference.

Now let's officially start the game. I'm using keyboard and mouse, so controls are based on that setup.

At the start of the game, Grace's FBI supervisor assigns her to investigate "Sawara Hotel." She accepts the assignment and arrives at Sawara City.

After the subtitles finish, we can start moving. Movement is WASD, and moving the mouse adjusts the camera direction.

Walk forward a short distance and you'll see a missing person poster on the newspaper stand to the right. The default interact key is F.

Walk a few more steps forward to trigger a cutscene. We explain our purpose to the police officer, and he grants us access to the crime scene at Sawara Hotel.

Once inside, keep going deeper. You'll need to press CTRL to crouch through here.

Go deeper until you see yellow crime scene tape at a doorway. Turn left and enter — that's our destination.

After pushing the door open, press G to turn on your flashlight.

Head to the kitchen ahead. Both the shelves on the left and the freezer in front can be interacted with.

The drawer next to it can also be opened, but there's nothing inside.

After investigating everything, go through the door on the left. There will be a short cutscene here.

After pushing through the door, the game prompts us to press TAB to open the menu and check the investigation report.

Find the report on the right side and open it to read.

After reading the report, investigate the floor ahead — you can see many footprints.

Investigate the table to see many claw marks.

There's a bathroom on the right side with things to investigate inside.

Coming out of the bathroom, we find a photograph on the pillar at the bar counter.

The person in the photo is us. You can flip the photo using the mouse or pressing Q/E. On the back, there's a key — press F to take it. There's also a message from a mysterious person telling us to go to Room 204.

The key is used to unlock the nearby door.

Open the door and head inside. There's something on the floor you can interact with.

It's a "Hotel Brochure" — this counts as a collectible item.

Files we've already read can be viewed again by pressing TAB in the "Files" menu. Some are useful for puzzles.

Continue further inside and enter through this door.

In the drawer, you can find a "Closure Notice."

On the wall, you can find the Hotel Map.

After exiting, the game prompts us to press M to open the map. You can check it anytime when you're not sure where to go.

After getting the map, go out and turn right, then head up the stairs.

At the top of the stairs, the door ahead can be interacted with, but we don't have the key yet.

To the left of the stairs, there's a pair of Wire Cutters. Pick them up.

With the Wire Cutters in hand, let's open the door on the left ahead.

Interact with the door, then select the Wire Cutters from the right side of the popup.

Keep going straight. The room ahead is Room 204, the one we're looking for. Inside, only a radio is playing — no one is there.

The radio can be turned off.

The drawer on the left is empty. Let's investigate the photo on the bed.

When looking at the photo, the game asks us to move the camera with the mouse. Move the camera upward — there's a white circle in the upper right corner you can interact with.

Unsurprisingly, all the photos on the bed are related to us — and they were all taken secretly.

Note: Minor spoilers ahead.

The game now shifts to 8 years ago — the night we were secretly photographed.

The other woman in the photo is Grace's mother, Alyssa. Suddenly the room lights go out, and her mother senses something is wrong.

We need to escape the hotel with Mom. Press SHIFT to run.

Keep following her. A cutscene will play ahead where we see Mom hiding something behind a painting.

Keep running after Mom. After the next cutscene, the scene returns to the present.

After another cutscene, we go look for what Mom hid.

We can also check the bathroom next to the bed to see if there's anything (just in case).

Now let's go back the way we came and head downstairs to find the painting. It's on the first floor — keep going down the stairs and look to the right.

Go ahead and interact with this shelf to push it aside.

After pushing it aside, interact with the painting.

Hidden behind the painting is a bag that can be opened.

First, take out the notebook.

Open it and read through it.

There's also a floppy disk inside the bag that you can take out.

Just as we finish interacting, the officer from earlier appears. He mumbles something about being pricked, and shortly after, turns into a zombie.

Now hold F to break free, and press A/D to dodge.

After breaking free, pick up the key on the ground.

You'll also need to press left mouse button to resist the zombie officer's attack.

After being bitten, head up the stairs and unlock the door that requires the key.

Enter the door, watch the cutscene, and this chapter ends.

At the end of the previous chapter, Grace was captured by the mysterious person. The scene now shifts to the other side.

After a cutscene, we can now control Leon. We need to chase down Victor — the mysterious person who captured Grace. He was formerly a T-virus researcher.

Walk forward a few steps and a female citizen will turn into a zombie. Press right mouse button to draw your weapon, left mouse button to aim.

Headshots are the most effective way to deal with zombies. (In this game, zombies that aren't killed with headshots may "revive" as more aggressive "Cauliflower Head" zombies.) When a zombie enters a staggered state, press F to follow up with a physical attack. (Leon has a high chance of destroying zombies with physical attacks, but Grace doesn't.)

Keep going down the road — there are many more zombies to deal with.

If a zombie is knocked down, we can perform a melee weapon attack from behind using the left mouse button.

When a melee weapon is equipped, left click is the attack button. A strong attack is Space + Left Click. The green bar on the left of the weapon indicates durability.

Some zombies will drop supply items.

When you reach the objective marker, the game prompts you to use the mouse wheel to switch to "Requiem." This weapon is extremely powerful but has limited ammo.

Use Requiem to quickly take down the zombies ahead.

In the following cutscene, we lose track of Victor. The mission handler directs us to find him at "Rose Mountain Sanatorium." At this point, Leon seems to have developed some "lesions" on his body.

The perspective now switches back to Grace.

Grace wakes up in an unfamiliar environment. There are many things to investigate nearby, but the main item is the "Medical Record" on the desk.

After checking everything in the room, go outside. There's a hallway — don't go through the door at the end yet.

First, go down this passage on the right.

There are some lights in the hallway you can turn on.

After turning on the lights, keep going forward, then turn left.

In the drawer at the end of the corner, you can find a "Cherubim Key."

After getting the key, go back. The first door at the corner — Room 201 — can be opened with this key.

You can find an Empty Bottle in the room's cabinet.

On the wall of the small room, there's a locked Fuse. We can't take it yet.

Pick up the Lighter nearby.

The Lighter can be used by pressing G.

Before leaving, you can manually save at the typewriter here.

After leaving, go left. Let's first check out Room 202 next door.

On the desk, you can find a book called "Shadow Ghost." You can read it (feels a bit like psychological suggestion).

On the table inside, there's a painting.

In the cabinet on the left, there's a "Note on a Cardboard Box."

The note contains information: "The Screwdriver is stored in the toolbox at the Nurse's Station."

After getting this note, go out and keep heading left. Enter the door at the end of the hallway.

Open the inner door and we'll see a massive Abnormal Zombie.

This zombie will start chasing us, but it's afraid of light. We can hide in the room ahead. (On Modern/Standard difficulty, you can survive one bite.)

If you run left immediately at the start, it will drop from above and block your path.

Once you see it climb up in the room, you can go out. (This room seems to only work as a hiding spot once — the second time it will turn off the lights.)

After leaving, keep going forward. Return to the room at the end of the hallway (where the Abnormal Zombie was earlier). You can pick up a Medical Syringe from the floor.

If your health is low, press Q to fully heal.

There are many things to investigate in the room, but our main target is the box up high.

To reach this box, we need to pull the cart on the left. In the cabinet to the left of the cart, there's a "Green Medicine."

When we pull the cart aside, we can see the Abnormal Zombie has returned outside.

Quickly climb up to get the toolbox. When you see the climb prompt, press F to climb.

Found the Screwdriver inside the toolbox.

After getting the Screwdriver, head toward the hallway with the horse sculpture. However, the Abnormal Zombie will burst through a door along the way.

At this point, you can go back to the room with lights (this time the lights will turn off).

Alternatively, use evasion tactics — circle around and get back to the hallway through the door it broke through. (Listen to the audio to track its position.)

For now, let's head to Room 201 in the hallway.

The game will remind us to press F to close the door. (Is it really useful?)

Enter the small room and use the Screwdriver we just got to remove the Fuse from the wall.

Getting the Fuse triggers a cutscene. The Abnormal Zombie takes an unexpected path — it comes down from the room where we got the Fuse. (So closing the door trapped us inside?)

After opening the door, quickly go right and install the Fuse.

Watch the next cutscene — Grace gets captured again.

The scene now shifts to Leon, who has arrived at the Rose Mountain Sanatorium mentioned earlier.

Once inside, a nurse guides us. Follow her.

While following the nurse to the operating room to wait for Victor, something unexpected happens. Unsurprisingly, the people in the Sanatorium have "gone mad," and we need to deal with the zombies attacking us.

This version of Leon can not only use firearms and physical attacks, but also parry by pressing Space at the right moment. It feels pretty badass.

After defeating the chainsaw zombie, the chainsaw will keep spinning on the ground and can still hurt you. Keep your distance.

Once the chainsaw completely stops, we can pick it up to use against zombies.

In the cabinet on the steps on the other side, there's a Mixed Herb.

Zombies will also come out of some doors that can't be opened. The chainsaw makes quick work of them.

The crates both inside and outside the room generally contain ammo.

There's also ammo in the cabinets.

After scavenging the room, go open the large door. This door can only be opened with the chainsaw.

After entering, keep walking forward until you reach a fireplace. On the mantle, there's a moving "Mr. Raccoon" figurine. Destroy it to collect it — this counts toward an achievement.

Continue all the way to the Sanatorium lobby. At the iron gate, our timeline converges with Grace's.

In the cutscene, Leon uses Requiem to blast the Abnormal Zombie with a few shots, and Grace is rescued.

However, after barely exchanging a few words, Victor's trap separates them. Leon hands a gun to Grace.

Leon is also captured by Victor.

The perspective switches back to Grace, at the moment she was separated from Leon.

Once you can control Grace, head downstairs first. The main door downstairs requires us to collect three items to open.

Knowing what to do, head up the stairs on the left.

Under the statue, there's a Green Herb. Use it to heal if your health isn't full.

After getting it, continue down the stairs. On the table downstairs, there's a Patient List.

Both ward areas are currently inaccessible — both require a keycard.

The map on the wall near the entrance can be interacted with.

The room across from the entrance can be entered — it's the only door we can access right now.

After entering, you can get an "Antique Coin" from the table on the right. (Antique Coins can be exchanged for items later.)

You can also save inside, and there's an Item Box on the right.

In the left corner of the room, there's medicine to pick up.

On the desk, you can find a "Sanatorium Brochure."

On the whiteboard, there are "Scrawled Notes on the Whiteboard." After getting this file, enter the door on the left.

After entering, we see a little girl locked inside.

While talking to the little girl, a male staff member lying on the ground tells us to stay away from her. He dies shortly after, and we obtain the "West Wing Keycard" from him.

Investigate his body to see that his neck was slashed by a sharp weapon.

The Level 3 Lock in the room can't be opened yet. Let's go out and explore for now.

After leaving the room, if you're not sure where to go, press M to open the map. The map information is quite detailed.

The West Wing door is to the left of the room entrance.

After pushing through the door, first head down the hallway to the left.

The door in the hallway also requires an item to open.

Keep going left. The door with the window leads to the kitchen.

Inside, there's a Chef Zombie busy at work.

We need to go through the door on the right side of the kitchen, but a cart in the kitchen aisle blocks our path. We need to push the cart all the way forward while the zombie has its back turned, cutting meat.

If you don't want to sneak, you can let the zombie push the cart for you. Pull the cart forward to block the path, and when it chases you, it'll push the cart forward.

Circle around with it in this area. You need it to push the cart twice to get it all the way forward.

After opening the kitchen door, there are two paths — left and right.

There's a zombie on the right side that keeps saying "there's a light left on." These types of zombies are sensitive to light. Let's go left first — don't enter the door on the right, keep going forward.

On the table on the left, there's a Simple Knife.

The drawer on the left requires a lockpick — it can't be opened yet. We came here mainly for the knife.

Now go back and enter the door on the left.

The zombie inside can be taken out with the knife we just got (Space + Left Click). After killing it, pick up the Herb behind it.

After getting the Herb, go out and head left. (There was a zombie here earlier — not sure where it went.)

There's a light on the wall you can turn on (up to you). There's a Herb on the ground to the right. The door on the right connects to the other side — you can ignore it for now.

Go left and enter this large door.

Behind the door, two zombies are happily eating meat at the table and couldn't care less about us. Pick up the gun on the ground.

After getting the gun, keep going deeper. Grace will say the gun has no ammo, and a zombie will spawn behind you. (What kind of mechanic is that?)

No ammo? What are you waiting for? Keep pushing through doors! The room on the left side of the hallway can be entered through the window or through the door.

There's a new Simple Knife in the cabinet.

Get ammo from the table.

After getting the ammo, a zombie will spawn in the room. You can fight it or skip it.

We still don't have access to the room on the left. Let's head out for now.

Go out and head left. There's an Antique Coin on the table.

There are stairs ahead. Break the crate under the stairs to get ammo.

At the end of the hallway, there's a large wrench.

Unsurprisingly, it's broken.

Nothing we can do about it. For now, let's head up the stairs.

Go up one floor. There's a map on the wall — interact with it to update your map.

The dark areas are places we haven't explored yet.

The door on the right is locked. We can only go through the door ahead.

Behind the door are three zombies. The two regular ones aren't too bad, but the singing zombie in the middle will stun-lock us with its song.

There's an Antique Coin on the piano — grab it first.

I recommend luring the two regular zombies over first and dealing with them.

The white-clothed zombie's scream will stun us for a moment. Keep your distance and you'll be fine.

After killing it, it drops another Antique Coin.

There's a Herb on the table over here.

There's a new gun on the bar counter.

There's a safe here, but it requires a combination. (We'll get the code later, but I'll write it here: ←10 →80 ←30. The code may be different on higher difficulties — check other guides.)

Note: Some players may not know how combination locks work. Here's a quick explanation.

First, turn to each number following the arrow direction. Don't reset (don't press F) — keep turning to the next number. After all three numbers are dialed in, press F to unlock.

Next, take the door on the other side. In the white coat hanging on the wall to the left, there's a "Folded Note."

The note contains new information.

There's ammo on the table to the right.

There's also a Herb on another table.

The door in the hallway can be pushed open directly. This is the "Chairman's Office" mentioned in the note.

Enter and look left. On the table, you can get a "Unicorn Ornament Box." Turn the box around and the back can be interacted with.

Interact to take out the Ruby inside. (This Ruby is used to open the door next to the kitchen.)

If it's too dark, you can turn on the lights first.

There's also a notebook on the desk you can interact with.

But reading it now shows no information — only traces of writing.

The machine on the right can also be ignored for now.

On the nearby table, there's a letter: "Email to a Manager (Printout)."

Continue further inside and pick up the pencil on the desk.

Deal with the zombie in the room.

In the trash can on the right, you can find a note: "Note to the Chairman."

This note needs to be opened and read.

The office desk drawer contains a few rounds of ammo.

With the pencil in hand, interact with the paper on the outside desk. Use the pencil to shade the paper and reveal the hidden text.

Then interact with the machine here.

Input in the order: "Moon, Sun, Star, Moon."

After opening, you receive the "Moon Crystal."

Open the door and go right to return to the lobby.

You can go to the lobby first and insert the "Moon Crystal" — otherwise it takes up an inventory slot.

After placing it, you can save inside the lobby. Then head through the east door to the Recreation Room we couldn't unlock earlier.

After arriving, insert the Ruby we got into the door slot.

Inside, there's an Antique Coin on the gambling table.

The display case on the left is where we spend Antique Coins.

Interact with the display case and insert the required number of Antique Coins to unlock items. The Fanny Pack costs 3 Antique Coins. I prioritized the Fanny Pack — after all, inventory space is always in crisis.

The three slots on the right are: "Stabilizer" — improves firearm handling and damage.

"Steroid" — increases maximum health.

"Matsaoka User's Guide" — increases Blood Collection Device capacity (+50). (I recommend getting this as your second purchase, as later crafting requires the increased capacity.)

There's another Antique Coin on the bar table inside.

Break this porcelain vase to get ammo.

You'll also break the jar on the table in the process — it contains another Antique Coin.
